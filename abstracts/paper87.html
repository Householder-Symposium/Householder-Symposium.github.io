Structure Preservation in Subspace Approximations Arising in Gauss Collocation Methods for DAEs

Andreas Frommer, Malak Diab, Karsten Kahl, Manuel Tsolakis

Abstract

Consider a linear differential-algebraic equation (DAE) of the form

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{0}\)

\begin{equation} \label {dae:eq} E\dot {x}(t) = Ax(t) + b(t), t\geq 0, \enspace x(0) = x_0, \end{equation}

where \(A,E \in \mathbb {R}^{n \times n}\), \(E \geq 0\) (positive semidefinite), \(x,b, \in \mathbb {R}^n\).

We are particularly interested in the cases where \(A\) is skew-Hermitian, \(A = -A^T\) or when \(A\) is negative real, \(A + A^T \leq 0\). These two cases correspond to dynamical systems which are energy-preserving or energy-dissipative, respectively, with energy measured by the \(E\)-seminorm, i.e. \(\|x(t)\| = \|x_0\|_E\) for all \(t\) or \(\|x(t)\|_E \downarrow \), respectively. A prominent example for the occurence of such DAEs appears within the port-Hamiltonian modelling of (coupled) dynamical systems.

When integrating (1) numerically, it is important to preserve energy conservation or dissipation as a crucial structural feature of the DAE. It was shown in [1, 3] that this is the case with the (implicit) Gauss-Legendre collocation methods.

An order \(s\) Gauss-Legendre collocation method obtains the approximation \(x(t+h)\) from that at \(x(t)\) as

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{1}\)

\begin{equation} \label {GL:eq} x(t+h) = (-1)^sx(t) + \sum _{j=1}^s\omega _j(hA-\tau _j)E)^{-1}Ex(t), \end{equation}

where the rational function \(r_s(z) = (-1)^sz + \sum _{j=1}^s\frac {\omega _j}{z-\tau _j}\) is the \(s\)-th diagonal Padé approximation to the exponential function.

To obtain \(x(t+h)\) in (2) one has to solve \(s\) linear systems, and when the dimension \(n\) is large, this might practically be done only iteratively.

The topic of this talk is thus to explore which iterative methods for (2) are at the same time eﬀicient and structure preserving, meaning that the iterates \(x^{(m)}\) approximating \(x(t+h)\) preserve energy if \(A = -A^T\) or have decreased energy if \(A+A^T \leq 0\).

In the case of an ODE, i.e. when \(E=I\), the following has been shown in [2].

  • Theorem 1 Let \(A = - A^T\) and \(E = I\). Then for any \(s\), the Arnoldi matrix function approximation \(x^{(m)}\) to \((-1)^sx(t) + \sum _{j=1}^s\omega _j(hA-\tau _j)I)^{-1}x(t)\) based on the Kryolv subspace generated by \(A\) and \(x(t)\) satsifies \(\|x^{(m)}\|_E = \|x(t)\|_E\).

For the DAE case and \(s=1\), where \(x(t+h)\) is the result of a generalized Cayley transformation, \(x(t+h) = \mathcal {C}(hA,E)x(t)\) with \(\mathcal {C}(hA,E) = (\tfrac {h}{2}A-E)^{-1}(\tfrac {h}{2}A+E)\), we have the following result.

  • Theorem 2 Let \(\mathcal {V}_m\) be an approximation subspace and define \(x^{(m)} \in \mathcal {V}_m\) as the Galerkin approximation which satisfies

    \[ (\tfrac {h}{2}A+E)x(t) - (\tfrac {h}{2}A-E)x^{(m)} \perp \mathcal {V}_m. \]

    Provided \(x(t) \in \mathcal {V}_m\), we then have:

    • (i) If \(A = -A^T\) then \(\|x^{(m)}\|_E = \|x(t)\|_E\).

    • (ii) If \(A + A^T \leq 0\) then \(\|x^{(m)}\|_E \leq \|x(t)\|_E\).

The theorem holds in particular if we obtain \(x^{(m)}\) as the iterates of an augmented FOM method for the linear system \((\tfrac {h}{2}A-E)x = (\tfrac {h}{2}A+E)x(t)\) with initial guess \(0\) and the Krylov subspace augmented with the span of \(x(t)\). Interestingly, it also holds for non-augmented FOM with the initial guess \(-x(t)\).

In our contribution we will discuss these results and present the current state of further results for higher order Gauss-Legendre collocation in the DAE case.
